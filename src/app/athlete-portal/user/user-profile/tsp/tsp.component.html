Your Training Stress Profile™ (TSP™) is a series of metrics that describe your ability to absorb different types of training stress over different time periods for each discipline. Each TSP metric ranges from 0 to 10 with 10 being the most training stress of a particular type, time period, and discipline.

TSP is far more effective at managing training stress than simplistic metrics that only measure average intensity over a total duration.

One athlete may be able to absorb a high level of one type of training stress in one session but not be able to do so very often. Another athlete may only be able to absorb a moderate amount in one session, but may be able to do so regularly.

Most of your PhysiFactors influence your TSP such as age, sport age, performance level, body compensation, weight, life stress, and so forth. Your TSP will also change over time as your body changes.

Types of Training Stress

The following are types of training stress included in your TSP and their respective zones as you may see them in your training schedule.

Aerobic (Zone 2)

Threshold (Zone 4)

Muscular (Zone 5)

Neural (Zone 6)

Time Periods

The following are time periods over which different training types are managed.

Session

Week (Microcycle - typically one week)

Month (Mesocycle - typically four weeks)

TSP Metrics

The following are TSP metrics used for each discipline.

ASpW - Aerobic Stress per Week

ASpM - Aerobic Stress per Month

TSpS - Threshold Stress per Session

TSpW - Threshold Stress per Week

TSpM - Threshold Stress per Month

MSpS - Muscular Stress per Session

MSpM - Muscular Stress per Month

NSpS - Neural Stress per Session

NSpM - Neural Stress per Month
